Around Italy, piazzas are social gathering places that function as the central hubs of the city. They are lively places filled with conversations, movement, and encounters with friends and strangers alike. However, due to the summer heat, the middle of piazzas are usually empty of people due to the lack of shade with people crowding around the edges of the square. Therefore, this unused space presents a unique opportunity to be activated while facilitating and enhancing the social interactions of the square.

This proposed urban furniture installation seeks to activate the unused spaces in the middle of piazzas by creating interactive lounge chairs which also provide shade for people to rest. Designed in an elongated U-shape, these chairs would be dispersed throughout a plaza, creating pockets of space which break up the established movement patterns in a square.

Sitting on one seat will raise the opposite tarp, providing shade for the person sitting across from you. Therefore, this chair requires a partner to function properly. This could be a friend, an acquaintance, or a complete stranger. Two people go in to escape the summer sun, but come out with a refreshed and enriched encounter.
